Understanding Learning Environments

Course at the School of Education where we learned the major theories of education developed over the course of the last century.
We developed concept maps summarizing these thinkers.
We also carried out observations of learning environments. I observed a Scout Troop meeting in Palo Alto.

  • Learn more

Beyond Bits & Atoms

Two-part course where we explored the use of maker spaces in education and learned about constructionism, the educational philosophy behind them. We learned to use technological tools to create educational products ourselves.
For one of my projects, my team built a pirate-themed board game that taught a student about the principles of programming.
In our final project, Shared Story, my team built a Tangible User Interface where young learners can record important life events in a collaborative map of their community, building empathy and understanding across peers from different backgrounds.

d.media

Digital design course at the d.school where we learned the basics of visual design and prototyping tools like Figma and InVision. Small projects included designing a restaurant menu and designing movie icons.
My main project involved designing a mobile and a desktop app for students in school projects to define and reflect on the skills they are learning

Internship: User Experience Research at Autodesk

In my internship I worked on user experience research for re-designing Autodesk's micro-credentialing system on their online learning content, specifically for Fusion 360.
I had to create and post surveys on the online learning platform and analyze the data, carry out interviews with university and community college students learning to use Fusion 360, and combine this quantitative and qualitative data into actionable insights.
